Two decades of food prices

Malaysia's food prices have largely stayed stable — until 2021, when global supply shocks changed everything.

Between 2010 and 2020, food inflation stayed under 4% annually. The post-pandemic period saw the sharpest sustained rise in recorded history.

Not all food inflated equally

Cooking oils, bread, and meat saw the steepest rises. Eating out became more expensive faster than buying groceries.

Where you live matters

Food costs are not the same across Malaysia. Urban states like KL and Selangor face higher prices, while East Malaysian states show different patterns.

Eating out is getting further from reach

The gap between cooking at home and eating out has widened — a trend that affects lower-income households most.

How Malaysia compares in Southeast Asia

Malaysia's food inflation sits in the middle of the regional pack — better than the Philippines, but worse than Singapore.
